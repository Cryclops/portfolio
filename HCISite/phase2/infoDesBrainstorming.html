Explanation of Ideas

Using the activities we came up with in our Activity Design process, we now started to think about how they would look on our software interface. We have incorporated some standard ideas, like the volume sliders and the play buttons. And we have incorporated some new features including a Music Cloud, buttons that allows changes to the frequency of the song, and display interfaces to change and play with the music.


In our information design brainstorming, we thought about how each feature would look on the screen. The buttons are going to be standard touch screen buttons that are big enough for the user to use. The sliders are going to be standard as well, and the user will use his/her finger to slide up and down or left and right.


The automation and equalizer features will be in a seperate Android "drawer." A new display screen will come up with the waveform of the song and the ability to draw on this waveform to change how the music sounds.


The smart library and the playlist features will be a seperate display screen. The library will have four different options; song, artist, album, and genre that the user can sort through by touching. The playlist feature will allow the user to queue up as many songs as he/she wants.

Overall, these features will fit nicely in our application. The sliders and buttons will be placed intelligently throughout the program. It will be easily navigated by any DJ.


Discussion with Client

After talking to our clients in our activtiy design phase, they were very eager to see the features we decided to implement. They wer excited to see the Music Cloud as well as all of the activities that would give the DJ the ability to change the sound (mash-ups, automation, equalizing, etc.)


We then met with our main client again after doing the information design process. He loved the way we decided to set up our Android application. The most intriguing idea that he seemed to really like was the equilizer. He loved that he could have the waveform of the song and be able to draw on top of it, playing only the part he drew on.


The second idea that our client liked was the volume and pitch sliders. But with our sketches, he did not like the fact that he could change both the volume and the frequency at the same time. Sometimes, this creates a cool effect. So we went back to our sketches and decided to move the pitch slider so that it overlaps some of the other buttons, giveing the DJ the ability to change both sliders.


Our client really enjoyed seeing our designs and gave us some excellent input. We took his advice, tweeked some of our ideas to match his, and put together a solid foundation for our interface.
