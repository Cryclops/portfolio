2.  Button Size


The width of the buttons has been expanded to fill the screen. This ensures that they are easy to hit, however does add the fear of accidentally hitting the wrong button. You can also see that the colors of the buttons have been changed to keep the design paradigm of color unitiy among individual song controls.


The buttons.


3.  Toast and the Pitch Slider (Added Affordances)


Since the text on the buttons was removed last phase, we were worried that first time users wouldn't initally understand what each icon implied the button did. As a result, we added something called Toast notifications. They are a non-interactive, semi-transparent text boxes that can show the user a simple line of text. They will show automatically for new users. However, the user can easily disable these notifications in the settings as they get used to the application.


We also added the pitch slider. Notice how it leaves both the left and right volume sliders accessable, while blocking the controls for the right side. Since this is a multitouch application, ideally the DJ would be able to control the left volume, the left pitch, and the right pitch all at the same time. Unfortunately, it turns out the Droid X doesn't support more than 2 simultanious touches. The pitch slider can be dismissed via the standard Android method of hitting the "Back" button, or touching any area that isn't a volume slider or the slider itself.


The rabbit and the turtle icons are used to show which direction the slider increases versus decreases pitch. We choose the rabbit because when the pitch of the song goes up, the song also gets faster, and the inverse for lowering the pitch. We felt that the rabbit and the turtle are common icons that afford a connotation about speed. One downside is that we weren't positive that this connotation would be universal around the world.


Lastly, the background of the pitch slider is different from that of our volume sliders. Why? Because we wanted to further distinguish that although they both slide, it will not impact the actual song volume. This is because we felt that if the volume of the song were to suddenly decrease, it would be a terrible error for the DJ.


The toast notification upon pressing the Left Pitch.


Overall, we feel that these additions bring the usability of our interface to an enjoyable level for our client-base. We feel that if implemented on fully multi-touch hardware, the implications of 3 or even 4 finger touch could be easily utilized in cool new ways by our application.
