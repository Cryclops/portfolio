Evaluation Goals


Goals


Ease of Use

  • Our first goal is the ease of use of our application. We want to evaluate how easy it is for a user to navigate, learn, and use our software.
    • How easy is our application to learn?
    • How easy is our application to use?

System Functionality

  • Our second goal is the overall functionality of our application. We want to evaluate how our application functions as compared to other competing applications.
    • Does our application fulfill the functionality requirements of a casual DJ?

Feature Depth

  • Our final goal is the feature depth of our application. We want to evaluate whether or not our application is more than just a DJ tool.
    • Do users feel like our application is more than just a novelty item?
    • Would users use this often?

Critical Aspects


Drawer

  • The buttons, sliders, and the drawer itself
    • Evaluation of the buttons and sliders in our drawer in regard to our three evaluation goals

Playlist Control/Flow

  • Creating, changing, and the flow of a playlist of music
    • Evaluation of a playlist in regard to our three evaluation goals

Home Screen

  • The song information and layout of our Home Screen
    • Evaluation of the song info and layout in regard to the three evaluation goals

AutoEQ Screen

  • Drawing on the waveform, playing with two songs at once in our AutoEQ screen
    • Evaluation of the drawing and playing with the waveform of a song in regard to the three evaluation goals


Metrics


One to Five Measuring Scale


Similar to the Likert Scale and the One-to-Ten Rating Scale, our rating scale ranges from 1 to 5. One is the lowest score possible and Five is the highest score a participant can give.

We will be using this scale to measure our evaluation tasks.


EXAMPLE


Rate your level of frustration during this task (1 = no frustration, 5 = most frustration).
