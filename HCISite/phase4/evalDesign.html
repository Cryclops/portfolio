Evaluation Design


Evaluation Type

  • Empirical
    • Observing and studying the users of our design through field studies

Please see our INFORMED CONSENT FORM document to understand how we conducted our evaluation.


Recruitment of Participant


  • Client-Type Participant
    • Stakeholders
    • Amateur or Professional DJs

  • Listener-Type Participant
    • Any person who listens to music
    • This person must know what a DJ is and what he does

Please see our BACKGROUND QUESTIONNAIRE document to understand what we expected from our participants.


Procedures


The following is a list of our procedures in our empirical evaluation. As well as the procedures, we also list the tasks we had our participants perform.


Procedures for Each Task


      1.     Show competing application sketch/prototype and ask them to do a task

      2.     Have participant fill out questionnaire on the task

      3.     Then, show our application sketch/prototype and ask them to do the same task

      4.     Have participant fill out the same questionnaire on the task


Tasks


      1.     While browsing the library of songs, pause the song as fast as you can (DRAWER TEST)

      2.     While in the Home Screen, changed the volume as fast as you can (HOME SCREEN, DRAWER TEST)

      3.     While in the Home Screen, change the EQ settings of the current song (AutoEQ TEST)

      4.     Add a song to a current playlist to the third position as fast as you can (PLAYLIST TEST)


Please see our INSTRUCTIONS/DATA COLLECTION FORM document to understand our procedures.


Metrics and Data Collection


To collect our data, we will ask our participants to fill out a questionnaire after each part of our task. In each question of our questionnaire, we will try to gather the following information.


What We Will Measure


      1.     Frustration (1-5 Scale)

      2.     Actual Time Taken (Seconds)

      3.     Mistakes Made (Quantity)

      4.     Additional Comments (Written Responses)


Please see our POST-TEST QUESTIONNAIRE document to understand the questions we asked after each task.


Data Analysis


The following is a list of what we are looking to analyze with the results we found in our Post-Test Questionnaires.


  • For each task we will compare our application to the competing application based on the average ratings we receive from our participants
  • We will try to find a trend in our data
    • Do our participants favor our application over the competing application?
    • Based on the results, are there any areas for improvement to the current prototype?
  • We will draw conclusions based on our results
